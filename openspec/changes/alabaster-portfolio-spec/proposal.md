## Why

Transition the portfolio from a generic "gallery of apps" to a "Technical Authority" showcase. This shift targets CTOs and technical leaders by highlighting deep engineering expertise (distributed systems, performance, reactivity) through a minimalist, high-spec "Alabaster Spec Sheet" aesthetic.

## What Changes

- **New Persona**: Establish the "Light Yagami" identity as a Web Systems Developer with expertise in the "hard paths" of engineering.
- **Visual Overhaul**: Implement the "Alabaster Spec Sheet" aesthetic—#faf9f5 base, intentional typography, industrial layout, and no "hacker" tropes.
- **Project Refocus**: Prioritize professional systems (Quix, Assistant Widget, ThriveDesk) over personal apps, focusing on architectural logic and performance.
- **Domain Authority**: Replace progress bars with categorized "Domain Authority" sections (Core Systems, Infrastructure, Architecture).
- **Architecture Over Imagery**: Use technical diagrams and high-spec typography to explain system logic where screenshots are insufficient.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities
- `information-architecture`: Define the sitemap and hierarchical structure of the technical spec sheet portfolio.
- `hero-statement`: Craft a high-impact, professional, and witty value proposition for the "Light Yagami" persona.
- `visual-design-system`: Establish the "Alabaster" design tokens, typography rules, and industrial layout patterns.

### Modified Capabilities
- None.

## Impact

- **Tech Stack**: Next.js (PPR), Tailwind CSS, GSAP.
- **Content**: Case studies for Quix, Assistant Widget, and ThriveDesk.
- **Tone**: Witty, unfiltered, professional, and "respect for the craft" oriented.
